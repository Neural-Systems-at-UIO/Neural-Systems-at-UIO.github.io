OBJECTIVES

To register all neuroscientific data to a common coordinate space

In coordination with other parts of the ebrains organisation we have several long-term objectives

  • Build tools that let scientists easily spatially register their data to a common coordinate space
  • Invest in platforms that let scientists share data
  • Create metadata models that enable shared data to be semantically mapped into knowledge space
  • Build visualisation tools for enabling a deeper understanding of registered data

REGISTER YOUR DATA

Register Your Histology With Our Online Workbench

We have developed an online workbench that allows scientists to spatially register their histology in a single, integrated workflow

  • Register to the Waxholm Space Atlas of the Sprague Dawley Rat Brain
  • Register to the Allen Mouse Brain Common Coordinate Framework
  • View your registered data in 3D space

OFFLINE WORKFLOW

Standalone Tools for Offline Use

You can complete the entire workflow offline using our standalone tools

  • Tools designed in collaboration with neuroanatomists
  • All our tools are open source, with publically available GitHub repositories
  • Contribute to the project or request new features through our repositories or contact forms

Atlas Registration

QuickNII

The first step in registering your data is finding the precise plane in the atlas which corresponds to your section

  • Available both offline and online
  • Simplifies complex anatomical analysis
  • Save your alignment files for sharing with others
  • Partially automated with deep learning using the DeepSlice neural network

Non-linear adjustments

Visualign

Since slide mounted sections often contain small deformations the fit to the atlas will initially not be perfect. This is why we built Visualign, a second step after QuickNII to correct for non-linear deformations.

  • Simple user interface
  • Supports both rat and mouse atlases
  • Available both online and offline

Transformation and visualisation

Nutil

You will need to segment your section using a tool such as Ilastik, then you are ready to transform your data into atlas space using Nutil!

  • Combine your alignments and segmentations
  • Available both online and offline
  • contains many utilities for working with full resolution microscopy datasets
  • Visualise the output in MeshView
